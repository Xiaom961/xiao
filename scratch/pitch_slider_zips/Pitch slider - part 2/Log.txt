Because we are playing back the character's speech using the browser's native Web Audio API inside Stage.vue, the possibilities are almost endless!

By default, the audio plays straight from the source to your speakers. But in Web Audio, we can insert any number of virtual "effects pedals" (called AudioNodes) in between them.

Here are some of the coolest effects we can easily implement in the future to make the character's voice sound incredibly premium or interactive:

1. 📻 Retro Walkie-Talkie / Vintage Radio Filter
If your character is acting like a radio host, a pilot, or transmitting from a secret base, we can make their voice sound like an old radio or walkie-talkie.

How it works: We chain a BiquadFilterNode (Highpass) to cut out all the deep bass, and another BiquadFilterNode (Lowpass) to cut out high treble, leaving only a retro mid-range telephone sound.
Vibe: Retro, mechanical, or military transmitter.
2. 🌌 High-Tech Sci-Fi / Cybernetic Droid Voice
If your character is an AI, a cyborg, or a mechanical droid, we can give their voice a metallic, futuristic cybernetic ring.

How it works: We can feed the audio through a Ring Modulator (multiplying their voice by a fast-oscillating sine wave) or use a very short feedback loop (a Comb Filter) to create a metallic, robotic resonant sound.
Vibe: Portal's GLaDOS, Star Wars droids, or high-tech AI.
3. ⛪ Room & Cathedral Reverb (Environmental Acoustics)
You can make the character sound like they are physically speaking to you from a cozy wooden cabin, a futuristic metal laboratory, a huge empty cathedral, or an auditorium.

How it works: We use a ConvolverNode loaded with a tiny audio sample of a room's acoustics (called an Impulse Response). The browser's Web Audio engine instantly calculates how the character's voice would bounce off the virtual walls!
Vibe: Immersive, realistic, and highly spatial.
4. 🔊 Spatial 3D Audio (3D Positional Audio)
Since the character is rendered as a 3D VRM or 2D Live2D avatar on your screen, we can link their voice to their actual position!

How it works: Using a PannerNode, we map the avatar's coordinates to the Web Audio 3D space.
If the character walks to the left side of your desktop screen, their voice will naturally come out of your left speaker/earbud.
If they step back or shrink, the sound becomes quieter and slightly echoey.
Vibe: Virtual Reality presence right on your desktop.
5. 🎤 ASMR / Intimate Whisper Mode
For quiet night-time chats, you can make the character's voice sound incredibly soft, warm, and intimate.

How it works: We roll off any sharp high-frequency sibilance (the harsh "s" and "t" sounds) using a low-pass filter and slightly compress the audio using a DynamicsCompressorNode to bring out the subtle warm breaths and whispers.
Vibe: Relaxing, cozy, and ultra-smooth.
🛠️ What the code would look like in Stage.vue:
Adding an effect is incredibly simple. For example, to make a Walkie-Talkie effect, we would just insert this into our existing playFunction:

javascript
// 1. Create the radio bandpass filters
const highpass = audioContext.createBiquadFilter()
highpass.type = 'highpass'
highpass.frequency.value = 500 // Cut out deep bass
const lowpass = audioContext.createBiquadFilter()
lowpass.type = 'lowpass'
lowpass.frequency.value = 3000 // Cut out crisp treble
// 2. Connect the chain: Source ➔ Highpass ➔ Lowpass ➔ Speakers
source.connect(highpass)
highpass.connect(lowpass)
lowpass.connect(audioContext.destination)
